Power BI: Property Market Analysis

This Power BI analytics project assess historical property transaction data from 2013–2019, focusing on market value, sale price trends, redevelopment potential, and data completeness. It equips urban planners, investors, and policymakers with strategic insights into land use, pricing hotspots, and future investment zones. We followed a structured, insight-to-strategy approach:Phase Activity Executive Output Data Acquisition & Validation Imported and cleaned 2013–2019 transaction records, validated completeness across actuals and forecasts Reliable historical dataset Exploratory Analytics Mapped sale price trends, transaction volumes, and redevelopment index movements Identified market inflexion KPI. Definition & DAX Modelling Created metrics: CAGR, price per acre, land use pricing tiers, completeness % Strategic performance indicators. Dashboard Build a focused report page: Market Overview, with a tooltip showing Data Completeness, Executive-ready insights pack Insight. Synthesis Distilled findings into strategic themes: pricing hotspots, redevelopment zones, and data reliability Actionable investment levers. The property market shows signs of value compression, redevelopment clustering, and land use pricing divergence. Key forces include: • Peak market activity in 2015, followed by a decline in both price and volume • Light Manufacturing and Mixed-Use zones outperforming other land types • High-value roads concentrated in redevelopment-prone areas • Incomplete data poses risks to forecasting and planning accuracy Strategic focus should be placed on redevelopment corridors, data integrity improvement, and land use optimisation.

Power BI Mobility Analytics Project

Executive Case Study (Q1 Pilot – Pretoria) Context & Objective RideUP launched a 3-month pilot bike-share programme across key Pretoria nodes to test commuter and leisure demand. This Power BI analytics project was initiated to: • Evaluate usage performance, demand patterns, and subscriber behaviour • Identify early commercial signals to inform scale-up decisions • Provide data-driven insights for executive strategy, capital allocation, and customer model design The scope covered 3 months of ride data (2 winter, 1 spring) and focused on establishing baseline operational intelligence for future expansion. How the Project Was Conducted This BI engagement followed a structured, C-Suite-oriented approach: Phase Activity Executive Output Data Acquisition & Validation Imported raw ride logs, cleansed data, created star-schema model (Trips, Calendar, Stations, Users) Reliable single source of truth Exploratory Analytics Identified seasonality, user segmentation, route patterns, time-of-day trends Found core demand levers KPI Definition & DAX Modelling Created C-Suite metrics: ride conversion, subscriber mix, duration uplift, station contribution, weekday vs weekend variance Enabled strategic dashboarding Dashboard Build Three focused report pages for Leadership: Performance, Behaviour, and Strategic Levers Executive decision pack Insight Synthesis Distilled analytics into 3–5 strategic themes with actions linked to commercial value Strategy-ready recommendations Market Context (Micro-Mobility) Pretoria has growing micro-mobility demand, driven by university and office commuting, but remains under-penetrated vs global bike-share benchmarks. RideUP competes for “short-hop urban mobility” share alongside: • Ride-hailing (Uber, Bolt) • Electric scooters (emerging pilots) • Private car/minibus taxi trips for < 5 km routes Early mover advantage exists, but scale requires habit-forming usage, safe routes, and pricing model optimisation.

Property Analytics Project – Insights to Strategic Decision-Making

Executive Case Study (Multi-Year Property Trends) Context & Objective UrbanScope initiated a multi-year property analytics project to evaluate evolving real estate dynamics across Gauteng. This Power BI engagement was designed to: • Analyse sale price trends, transaction volumes, and land use shifts • Identify value compression, market volatility, and occupancy patterns • Support strategic planning, investment decisions, and urban policy formulation The scope spans 2015–2021, integrating transactional, locational, and structural data to build a comprehensive urban property intelligence framework. How the Project Was Conducted This BI initiative followed a structured, executive-aligned methodology: Data Acquisition & Validation Consolidated multi-source property data, normalised sale prices, built time-series and categorical models Trusted analytics foundation Exploratory Analytics Mapped transaction count volatility, CAGR trends, and land use evolution Identified market inflexion KPI Definition & DAX Modelling Created metrics: price per bedroom/bathroom, land share %, vacant % total, volatility index Strategic performance indicators Dashboard Build Four thematic report pages: Performance, Behaviour, Land Use, and Value Compression Executive-ready insights pack Insight Synthesis Extracted key themes: price decline drivers, occupancy shifts, and land use concentration Actionable strategic levers Market Context (Gauteng Property Landscape) Gauteng’s property market reflects urban densification, land use diversification, and price volatility. Key forces shaping the landscape include: • Shifting buyer preferences (e.g., apartments vs detached homes) • Government zoning and infrastructure projects • Vacancy rates and underutilised land segments • Macro-economic pressures on sale price growth Strategic opportunities lie in land repurposing, mixed-use development, and targeted investment in high-demand corridors.
